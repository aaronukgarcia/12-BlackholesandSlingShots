### 1. The Propulsion Asymmetry
We know that gravitational slingshots work.
* **Jupiter:** ~26 km/s boost (0.00009c). Useful for Voyager, useless for stars.
* **Black Hole Binaries:** Theoretical boosts of **0.01c ‚Äì 0.1c** using standard gravity, or potentially **0.13c** using the [Kipping Halo Drive](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.03423) mechanism (mirroring photons off the event horizon).

### 2. The Deceleration Problem
The same physics that makes black holes excellent accelerators makes them critical for **braking**.
* Accelerating to 0.1c is "easy" with a black hole departure.
* **Stopping** at a destination *without* a black hole requires carrying enough fuel to cancel that relativistic momentum.
* The Rocket Equation makes this practically impossible (mass ratios > $10^{400}$).

**Conclusion:** Efficient relativistic travel is constrained to a node-to-node graph. Destinations *off* the graph (like Earth) are cul-de-sacs.
